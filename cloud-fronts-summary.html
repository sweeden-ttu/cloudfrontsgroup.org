About Cloud Fronts Group

Central Texas Technology Access & Innovation Delivery Partnership

Cloud Fronts Group was formed to bridge the gap between advanced research capabilities and real community needs in Central Texas. We operate as a technology access and innovation delivery partnership, bringing together expertise in AI/ML research, government program analytics, and community technology deployment.

Our work is organized around four core areas:

⚙

AI/ML Research

Theoretical and applied AI safety research, including our AAAI 2026 publication on bounded-agent security guardrails for government intelligence reporting.

📊

HUD Data Analytics

Analysis of HUD NOFO datasets, housing market trends, demographic indicators, and program effectiveness metrics for public-sector clients.

🏛

Section 3 Compliance

Comprehensive Section 3 compliance support for housing authorities, including strategic planning, benchmarking, reporting, and technical assistance.

🚀

Technology Access

Community technology access programs, innovation delivery services, and civic technology tools that make advanced capabilities available to underserved communities.

Research & Publication

Authors: Dr. Akbar Namin (Analysis), Scott Weeden (Mathematical Foundations), Johnathan King (Red Team Security) — Cloud Fronts Group

This paper introduces a formal framework for bounded-agent security guardrails, combining theoretical computer science with practical AI safety requirements for government intelligence reporting systems.

Community Analytics

HUD NOFO Data Analysis

We analyze housing, farm, and business growth indicators across Central Texas, the Western District, and the New Mexico border region. Our analytics capabilities include:

  • Housing inventory and price trend analysis
  • Emerging market identification
  • Tax abatement scenario modeling
  • City budget and demographic analysis
  • Foreclosure market intelligence

Data-Driven Policy: Our analytics are designed to support evidence-based decision-making for local governments, housing authorities, and community organizations across the Central Texas region.
