Rental Assistance Programs

If you need help paying rent or finding affordable housing in Central Texas, several federal, state, and local programs can help. Below is a guide to the major options and how to apply.

Housing Choice Voucher Program (Section 8)

The largest federal rental assistance program. Participants find their own housing (house, apartment, townhouse) and pay roughly 30% of their income toward rent; the voucher covers the rest up to a payment standard.

  • Administered by: Local Public Housing Agencies (PHAs)
  • Wait times: Often 1–3 years; many PHAs have closed waitlists
  • Apply at: Your local PHA — see contacts below

Public Housing

Rent in PHA-owned apartments at below-market rates. Income limits apply; rent is capped at 30% of adjusted income.

  • Apply at: Local PHA housing authority office
  • Wait times: Varies widely by city and unit size

VASH Vouchers (Veterans)

HUD-VASH combines a Section 8 voucher with VA case management for homeless veterans. Contact the VA Central Texas Health Care System at (254) 297-3000 or your local VA social worker.

LIHTC Properties

Low-Income Housing Tax Credit apartments offer income-restricted rents. Not a voucher — you apply directly to the property owner. See our LIHTC page for details.

Emergency Rental Assistance (Treasury / State)

Short-term help paying rent and utilities for households behind on rent due to financial hardship. Funding is distributed through local counties and nonprofit partners.

  • Texas Department of Housing and Community Affairs (TDHCA): tdhca.state.tx.us/rental-assistance
  • Bell County: Contact Bell County Health & Human Services at (254) 933-5380
