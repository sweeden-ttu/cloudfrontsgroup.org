Understanding USDA Farm Loans for Central Texas Producers

The USDA Farm Service Agency (FSA) offers a range of loan programs designed to help agricultural producers who may not qualify for conventional financing. For small and beginning farmers in Central Texas, these programs can be a critical resource for acquiring land, equipment, and operating capital.

Direct Farm Ownership Loans

FSA direct loans are funded directly by the USDA. The borrower works with FSA staff throughout the lifecycle of the loan. Key features include:

  • Maximum amount: $600,000 (adjusted annually for inflation)
  • Interest rate: Variable — typically below market rate, set quarterly by FSA
  • Eligibility: Must be a family farm operator unable to obtain credit elsewhere
  • Use: Purchase farmland, construct or repair buildings, improve soil and water conservation

Direct Farm Operating Loans

Operating loans cover annual production costs and general farm expenses. They are typically structured as term loans or lines of credit:

  • Maximum amount: $400,000
  • Use: Purchase livestock, seed, feed, equipment, fuel, insurance, and other operating expenses
  • Repayment: Up to 7 years (annual crops) or longer for multi-year enterprises

Guaranteed Loans

Under the guaranteed loan program, FSA guarantees up to 95% of the loan amount made by a commercial lender. This reduces risk for banks, making them more willing to lend to beginning or underserved producers:

  • Maximum amount: $2,096,350 (adjusted annually)
  • Use: Same as direct loans — farm ownership, operating, and improvement
  • Lender: Any commercial or cooperative lending institution

Microloans

The FSA Microloan program is designed for small, beginning, or niche farmers with less capital-intensive operations:

  • Maximum amount: $50,000
  • Streamlined application: Simplified paperwork and faster processing
  • Use: Startup costs, equipment, supplies, marketing, and operating expenses

Emergency Loans

Following natural disasters, FSA Emergency Loans help producers recover and rebuild:

  • Eligibility: Must be in a county declared a primary disaster area
  • Use: Restore damaged property, replace livestock, refinance existing debt
  • Amount: Up to $500,000 at reduced interest rates

How to Apply

Applications are processed through local FSA county offices. Producers should prepare:

  • Farm operating plan and financial statements
  • Production history and projected income
  • Personal financial statement
  • Entity documentation (if applying as an LLC or corporation)

For the Central Texas area, the Bell County FSA office (254-933-5300) serves Falls, Bell, and surrounding counties. The USDA service center locator at offices.sc.egov.usda.gov can identify your local office.
