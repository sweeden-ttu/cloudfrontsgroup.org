Section 3 Compliance Guide for Housing Authorities

Section 3 of the Housing and Urban Development Act of 1968 is a critical but often misunderstood program requirement for public housing authorities (PHAs). This guide provides a practical overview of Section 3 compliance obligations, benchmarking requirements, and best practices for PHAs seeking to meet HUD's regulatory standards.

What is Section 3?

Section 3 is a statutory requirement that ensures that economic opportunities generated by HUD-funded projects — including public housing construction, rehabilitation, and operations — are directed to low- and very low-income residents, particularly those living in or near the project area. The program creates a pipeline for employment, training, and contracting opportunities for Section 3 residents and businesses that employ them.

Who Must Comply

Section 3 applies to PHAs and other recipients of HUD financial assistance exceeding $200,000 in a program year. Covered programs include:

  • Public Housing Capital Fund
  • Operating Fund
  • Choice Neighborhoods
  • Community Development Block Grants (CDBG) — when used for housing or community development activities
  • HOME Investment Partnerships Program

Key Requirements

Section 3 establishes three core compliance areas:

  • Hiring: PHAs must ensure that Section 3 residents receive priority for employment opportunities generated by HUD-funded projects. The current benchmark requires that at least 10% of aggregate workforce hours be filled by Section 3 residents.
  • Contracting: At least 3% of the total dollar amount of all Section 3-covered contracts must be awarded to Section 3 business concerns.
  • Training: PHAs must document efforts to provide training and apprenticeship opportunities that lead to permanent employment for Section 3 residents.

Benchmarking and Reporting

HUD evaluates Section 3 compliance through annual reporting in the VMS-PIC (or successor) system. Key reporting elements include:

  • Total number of labor hours worked in connection with Section 3-covered projects
  • Number and percentage of hours worked by Section 3 residents
  • Total dollar amount of Section 3-covered contracts awarded
  • Number and dollar amount of contracts awarded to Section 3 business concerns
  • Description of outreach and training activities conducted

PHAs that meet or exceed the 10% hiring and 3% contracting benchmarks are considered compliant. Those falling below must submit a corrective action plan.

Strategic Planning Process

An effective Section 3 compliance strategy typically involves:

  • Needs Assessment: Analyzing current Section 3 performance and identifying gaps
  • Benchmarking: Setting realistic but ambitious targets for hiring and contracting
  • Outreach: Developing partnerships with workforce development boards, community colleges, and local hiring halls
  • Monitoring: Tracking Section 3 metrics in real time throughout each project
  • Reporting: Preparing accurate and timely annual reports with supporting documentation

Common Compliance Pitfalls

  • Inadequate recordkeeping: Failing to track Section 3 resident hours and contract awards at the project level
  • Lack of outreach documentation: Not maintaining records of Section 3 recruitment and notification efforts
  • Contract language: Omitting Section 3 clauses from procurement documents and contracts
  • Missed corrective action deadlines: Failing to submit or implement corrective action plans when benchmarks are missed
  • Staff turnover: Losing institutional knowledge about Section 3 processes when key staff depart
